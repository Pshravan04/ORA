THE REALITY

Inspiration is easy.
Execution is hard.

Most people spend months collecting ideas — and then face a completely different world when it's time to actually build. Here's what typically goes wrong:

Budgets that keep moving

What starts at 8L ends at 14L. Unexpected costs arrive after work has already started — when walking away isn't an option.

Timelines that stretch

"2 months" turns into 5. You're living out of boxes, working from disrupted offices, or delaying your opening.

Workmanship that disappoints

The gap between what you were shown and what you received. Redone work. Renegotiated bills. Lingering regret.

No single point of accountability

Contractors, vendors, and suppliers operating without coordination. You become the project manager — without the expertise.
